software dev reflections

I won't dwell on the technical details of the course, you can read it here.

In many ways, this course forced me to mature as a human being. Many times in my academic career, I would rely on the forgiveness of my teachers and TAs to help me.

This course was the first time in my life that I had doubted my capabilites, significantly.

I remember on my birthday, there was this incredibly hard milestone due within 48 hours. I never celebrated my birthday, instead I was cooped up with my partner in the depths of hayden hall, puzzled at the homework specification.

I remember the times when I went up in front of the class, prepared to show off the code I had so painstakingly designed and written over hours of hardwork, only to get ripped to shreds by the professor. Forced to redesign and think about a new approach to the problem.

I remember working so late into the night, realizing that I hadn't eaten the entire day. No shops were open, the streets were quiet, and the air felt frigid when I left the building.

But...

I remember laughing with my partner those late nights. Joking about why we were taking this class and how our lives would be so much simpler if we hadn't

I remember submitting the final milestone, hugging my classmates and partners, just relieved that it was all done.

I remember the final code walk, where I slurred my words and trembled in front of the professor. Going over code that I hadn't practiced before. I received the highest score I had ever received in a code walk, an 8/10.

I remember many things about this class, and will continue to remember them for a long time.
